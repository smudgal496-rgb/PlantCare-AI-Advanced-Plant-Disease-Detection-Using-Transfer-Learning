          PlantCare-AI: Advanced Plant Disease Detection Using Transfer Learning
                                      Project Documentation

1. Introduction

Agriculture plays a vital role in the global economy and food security. However, plant diseases significantly 
reduce crop yield and quality. Early and accurate detection of plant diseases is essential to minimize crop losses.

Traditional disease detection methods rely on manual inspection by experts, which is time-consuming, expensive,
and not always accessible to farmers. With the advancement of Artificial Intelligence and Computer Vision, 
automated disease detection systems can help farmers identify plant diseases quickly and accurately.

The project PlantCare-AI aims to build an intelligent system capable of detecting plant diseases from leaf images 
using deep learning techniques. By leveraging transfer learning, the model utilizes pre-trained convolutional 
neural networks to achieve high accuracy even with limited datasets.

The system allows users to upload plant leaf images and receive predictions about possible diseases along with 
recommendations for treatment.

2. Problem Statement

Farmers often struggle to identify plant diseases at early stages due to lack of expertise and resources. 
Misdiagnosis can lead to excessive pesticide usage, reduced productivity, and economic losses.

Therefore, there is a need for an automated, accurate, and accessible solution that can detect plant diseases 
using image analysis.

The objective of this project is to develop an AI-powered plant disease detection system that can classify 
diseases from leaf images using transfer learning.

3. Objectives

The main objectives of the project are:

● To develop an automated plant disease detection system.

5. Literature Review

Several researchers have worked on plant disease detection using machine learning and deep learning techniques.

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) have proven effective for image classification tasks. Researchers have 
applied models such as:

(a) VGG16

(b) ResNet

(c) MobileNet

These models are often used with transfer learning to improve performance on agricultural datasets.

Studies show that transfer learning significantly reduces training time and improves accuracy, especially when 
datasets are limited.
