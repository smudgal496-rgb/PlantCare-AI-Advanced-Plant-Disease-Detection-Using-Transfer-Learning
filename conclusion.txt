The PlantCare AI system demonstrates the effectiveness of applying transfer learning with deep learning models such
as MobileNetV2 or other CNN architectures for plant disease detection. The system successfully analyzes leaf 
images and classifies them into healthy or diseased categories, helping in early identification of plant diseases. Transfer learning allows the model to utilize knowledge from pre-trained networks trained on large datasets, which significantly improves accuracy and reduces training time even when limited agricultural datasets are available.

The implementation of this system shows that AI-based disease detection can outperform traditional manual 
inspection methods that are time-consuming and prone to human error. By using image processing and deep learning, 
the system provides a faster and more reliable method for detecting plant diseases. Early detection enables farmers
to take preventive measures, reducing crop loss and improving agricultural productivity.

Overall, PlantCare AI contributes to the development of smart agriculture by integrating artificial intelligence 
with crop monitoring. The system has the potential to support farmers, agricultural experts, and researchers by 
providing accurate and automated disease diagnosis, thereby improving crop health management and food security.
